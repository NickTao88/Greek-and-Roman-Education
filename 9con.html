Power

“To have command is to have all the power you will ever need. To have all the power you will ever need, is to have the world in the palm of your hand. Look, Mr. Arduum, you are in charge of the school, this I do not doubt. Yet, just know that you never had power over my life. You were never in command. You never had the world in your palm. I will go on to become a great orator and pursue politics, even if you detest it. When I rise as emperor, it will be me who has the world in the palm of my hand! Nothing can stop me from being an excellent politician!”
you argue, fueled by the frustration toward this oppressive teacher for the past four years and shocked that he would deny your potential. (Again, Tiberius actually said the quote “to have command… the world in the palm of your hand.” Yet, here it is used fictionally to achieve self-encouragement.)
“Well, then I will surely see to it that you do actually get into politics. Mind you, politics is tough, Mr. Nero.” Mr. Arduum says tonelessly.
